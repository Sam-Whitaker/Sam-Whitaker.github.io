About

Hello! I'm Sam, a postgraduate research student and PhD candidate at Newcastle University.

I'm currently in the process of completing my PhD research, which is all about devoloping Bayesian inference schemes for use on large scale epidemics. In particular, I'm interested in seeing what we can learn about the underlying dynamics of an epidemic when provided with incidence data. Up to now, I've worked with data from a range of disease outbreaks, including COVID-19.

My usual day-to-day is often spent coding up complex algorithms in R or Python but, when I get a break from running countless simulations, I like to keep my brain stimulated with one of my many hobbies: enthusiast wildlife photographer, beginner banjo player, casual gamer, avid Burnley Football Club supporter, huge nature lover and now website creator. I'm always keen to try new things which, coupled with my passion for learning, means there's always a chance that a new hobby of mine is just around the corner!
